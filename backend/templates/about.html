{% block title %} Preliminary case file analysis {% endblock %}

Case files and adjudication orders have a lot of information that are not present in the regulations. These are often used as precedents when a similar situation arises, and thus for any downstream task such as penatly prediction it becomes important to understand and extract the information present in the case files. Similarly, regulations are also explored through the usage of case files which lets you know the scope of the regulations and how and where it is applied. Thus we have two different knowledge bases: regulations and case files, and it becomes necessary for systems to leverage this information.

In this webapp, we visualise the different case files and adjudication orders associated with each regulation. Based off of Swati's eariler work on understanding case files, we have annotated and built systems to tag the case files according the following given tags

  • Material fact
  • Vioaltion
  • Prodcedural fact
  • Allegations
  • Issues framed
  • Statuatory fact
  • Defendent claim
  • Subj observation
  • Rel fact
  • Penalty
We provide a focussed view of the case file where we only show parts of the case files that are likely to be relevant for legal teams based. We explore the different perspectives of the stakeholders involved.

Lawyers are the people arguing on behalf of the comapny against the SEBI and their arguments and ideas are denoted by the following tags

  • Procedural Fact
  • Defendent Claim

SEBI perspective explored the arguments made by the lawyers on behalf of SEBI and their arguments are denoted by the following tags

  • Material Facts

Judges perspective expolred the overseeing authority of the case and their tags are marked with

  • Subjective Observation
  • Penalty
